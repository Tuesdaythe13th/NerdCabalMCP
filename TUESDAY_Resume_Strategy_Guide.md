## üöÄ Pro Tips

### 1. The "Research Sample" Strategy
Always have 1-2 research samples ready:
- AI Psychosis research (1-pager)
- Neural Forensic Suite (Colab notebook)
- Benchmark design case study

Offer to share in cover letter: "I'd be happy to share my research on [topic] if helpful."

### 2. The "I Read Your Paper" Opener
If applying to research lab:
"I recently read your work on [specific paper] and was particularly intrigued by [specific finding]. My research on [your work] explores a complementary angle..."

**This separates you from 95% of applicants.**

### 3. The "Warm Intro" Hack
Check LinkedIn for:
- People who worked at target company
- MLCommons connections
- Conference co-presenters
- Co-authors on collaborative work

Ask for intro or at least advice: "I'm considering applying to [role] at [company]. Based on your experience there, would you recommend it?"

### 4. The "Demonstrate Value Immediately" Approach
In cover letter, offer something:
- "I noticed your team is working on [X]. I recently developed a framework for [related Y] that might be relevant."
- "I'd be happy to share my evaluation rubric for [domain] if useful."

**Give value before asking for job.**
